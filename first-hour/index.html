How is this scored?

The fifty-four core items use a four-point Likert anchor: Almost never (1), Some weeks (2), Most weeks (3), This is how I live (4). The six final agency-matrix items are binary YES/NO. The instrument is observational, not aspirational.

Nine chambers (Money, Time, Attention, Body, Mind, Word, Promise, People, Spirit) each carry six items. They group into three triads: The Stolen Resources, The Inner Ruins, The Broken Vessels. Eight chambers are scored as drift; the ninth, Spirit, is scored as readiness pulse. We classify the loudest drift chamber at quiet, active, or loud based on the percentage of the chamber's possible score (1–4 per item).

The cost-of-autopilot figures (hours/year on autopilot, promises broken, conversations postponed, money operating outside conscious choice) are calibrated to the autopilot literature. Killingsworth and Gilbert (2010) found that adults spend ~47% of waking time on autopilot, and our multipliers track that ceiling. Figures are estimates derived from your responses, not measurements.

The diagnosis is the door, not the destination.
