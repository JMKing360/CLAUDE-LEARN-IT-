3. How we collect it

  • Directly from you, when you complete an assessment, send an email, register for a session, or join a cohort.
  • Locally on your own device, through the assessment instruments, which save your answers and report to your browser’s local storage so you can return to your path.
  • Through email-delivery infrastructure, when you choose to send your report by email. The email containing your report is generated client-side and routed through our customer-relationship platform (currently GoHighLevel) operating under contractual data-processing terms. A copy of the email — name, email address, your free-text reflections (where you have entered them), and your assessment scores — is also delivered to an archive mailbox controlled by Dr. Mogire so that we retain a record sufficient to deliver continuity of care across cohorts and to respond to participant questions weeks or months after the assessment. You can request deletion of that copy at any time by writing to mail@mogire.com with the words delete my record in the subject line.

When personal information is transferred from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, we rely on one or more of the following safeguards as required by Articles 44 to 49 GDPR:

  • The European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses (or the UK International Data Transfer Addendum) with each non-EEA recipient;
  • An adequacy decision where one applies;
  • Your explicit consent, where appropriate and after receiving the information required by Article 49(1)(a).

You may request a copy of the relevant safeguard at any time by writing to mail@mogire.com.

Sale and sharing

We do not sell your personal information for monetary consideration. Loading the Meta Pixel, however, qualifies as a “share” within the expanded definition of California Civil Code §1798.140(ah) because it discloses limited online-activity information (page URL, event names, Meta-managed cookies, IP-derived location) to Meta Platforms, Inc. for cross-context behavioural advertising.

To exercise your right to opt out of this sharing you have three independent paths, any of which is sufficient:

  • Send a Global Privacy Control signal from your browser. We honour Sec-GPC: 1 at the edge: the Pixel and its noscript fallback are stripped from the page before it reaches your browser.
  • Use a browser that resides in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland. We suppress the Pixel for those regions by default.
  • Email mail@mogire.com with the words California opt-out in the subject. We will record the request and confirm by reply.

We do not retaliate or change our service for users who opt out.

12. Cookies and similar technologies

The assessment instruments themselves use your browser’s local storage to save your assessment history on your own device; they do not set first-party tracking cookies of their own. The website may, where deployed, set strictly necessary cookies (for example, to maintain a session).

Meta Pixel. The websites in our property network include the Meta (Facebook) Pixel for measurement of campaign performance and to build audiences for direct marketing. Two Pixel identifiers are deployed across the network:

  • Pixel ID 748998691952331 — the primary House of Mastery pixel, deployed on hom.mogire.com (the instruments hub including The First Hour and KOORA UNFINISHED) and on the marketing pages of houseofmastery.co.
  • Pixel ID 214369085789 — a secondary pixel deployed specifically on houseofmastery.co/diagnostic (the 9-pattern Unfinished Life Diagnostic). It carries the same suppression rules (EEA/UK/CH, Sec-GPC) and the same advanced-matching posture as the primary pixel.

Where either pixel loads, it sets and reads cookies managed by Meta Platforms, Inc. (principally _fbp, and _fbc when you arrive via a Meta-served ad). The Pixel transmits the following to Meta on each page view or milestone event:

  • The page URL (we strip any name, email, first, last, and token query parameters before the Pixel is allowed to read the URL, so retake links from email do not leak personal identifiers);
  • The event name — PageView, ViewContent, the standard events Lead (you confirmed your email at the gate) and CompleteRegistration (you reached your assessment result), and our custom milestone events (such as the welcome screen being shown, the begin button being clicked, mid-assessment progress thresholds, and section transitions);
  • The custom event properties we attach — the instrument identifier (koora or first-hour), the assessment’s content identifier, the question index at the moment of the event, and the synthetic conversion value used for ads optimisation;
  • Meta-managed first-party cookie identifiers (_fbp, _fbc) and any fbclid click identifier present in the URL;
  • The browser’s document referrer, screen dimensions, language, time-zone offset, and an IP-derived approximate location.

We do not transmit your name, email, phone number, or assessment answers to Meta. We have initialised the Pixel with autoConfig: false, which disables Meta’s automatic configuration polling — the mechanism through which Automatic Advanced Matching would otherwise sniff and hash form-field values from the page. We do not load the Pixel for visitors connecting from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, and we suppress it at the edge for any visitor whose browser sends a Global Privacy Control signal (Sec-GPC: 1) — in those cases, neither the Pixel script nor its non-JavaScript image fallback is present in the page you receive. We also suppress the Pixel when the assessment is loaded inside an embedded iframe.

You can opt out of Meta’s ads-personalisation through your Meta account settings, your browser’s cookie controls, the Global Privacy Control standard (which we honour), and the industry opt-out tools at aboutads.info/choices and youronlinechoices.eu. Meta’s own data practices are governed by the Meta Privacy Policy.

We will publish a separate cookie notice if and when our use of similar technologies materially changes.
