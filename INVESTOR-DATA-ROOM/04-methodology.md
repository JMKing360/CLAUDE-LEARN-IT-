# Methodology

The diagnostic instruments of House of Mastery are derived from the KOORA Master Architecture, refined against verbatim language from public discourse and peer-reviewed psychological research. This document is the methodology summary fit for review by clinical, academic, or commercial diligence.

## Item architecture

Every item is written as a **first-person, present-tense pattern observation**: *"When X, I [pattern]."* Items are deliberately observational rather than evaluative to lower defensiveness.

Every item carries **three tier variants**:

- **Tier 0** (start): names the wound
- **Tier 1** (middle): names the noticing
- **Tier 2** (end): names the new capacity

The tier resolves from where the participant is on the program path:

- **The First Hour**: tier rises by one with each completed return (six retakes over six months)
- **KOORA**: tier rises with chronological day across the 180-day cycle

## Scale

Four-point Likert with concrete frequency anchors:

- 1 = Almost never
- 2 = Some weeks
- 3 = Most weeks
- 4 = This is how I live

Recall window held constant within an instrument.

## Scoring

- Subscale scores are unweighted sums normalised to percentage of maximum
- Three-band classification with cutpoints at 0.44 and 0.69 of subscale maximum
- Negative items label as Quiet / Active / Loud
- Positive items label as Beginning / Practicing / Embodied

Scoring is deterministic. Same answer pattern always produces the same result. Unit tests will be added in Phase 5 to lock this property.

## Cost estimation

Cost figures shown to participants are derived from their own answers using transparent linear maps documented inline in `estimateCost()`. The estimates are deliberately conservative and clearly framed as estimates rather than measurements. The five-year forecast multiplies the annual estimates and is accompanied by the explicit caveat *"if the pattern continues at this rate."*

## Test–retest design

Both instruments are designed for repeated administration:

- The First Hour every 30 days for six months
- KOORA every 15 days across the 180-day cycle (13 returns plus alumni continuation)

Movement between returns is surfaced on the dashboard, in the result block, and in the email payload, supporting longitudinal sensitivity to change.

## Validity claims

We make no clinical-diagnostic claims. The instruments are described as **diagnostic instruments of life pattern**, not as clinical assessments of mental-health conditions. Result language uses *"your pattern suggests"* rather than diagnostic labels.

Convergent theoretical reference:

- The four-reflex construct overlaps theoretical territory with rumination (Nolen-Hoeksema), self-criticism (Gilbert), social comparison (Festinger and modern variants), and approval-status orientation
- The four faculties draw on self-regulation literature on attention, agency, and behavioural integrity
- Procrastination framing follows the Sirois / APA tradition: emotion regulation, not laziness or time-management failure
- The Zeigarnik effect (Wendsche et al., 2026) is referenced in the cost language for unfinished tasks

## Validation roadmap

- Internal consistency: target Cronbach α ≥ 0.70 per subscale once N ≥ 100
- Item discrimination: monitor item-total correlations
- Test–retest reliability: assess after 90-day cohort
- Convergent validity: planned correlation study against established measures

## Ethical considerations

- Items written to be shame-resilient, framing patterns as recoverable rather than as character defects
- Free-text reflections are optional and persist only with the participant's consent (the Send Results action)
- No clinical-diagnostic claims are made
- Children under 16 are not the intended audience
- Privacy policy compliant with GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA / CPRA, Kenya DPA, LGPD
