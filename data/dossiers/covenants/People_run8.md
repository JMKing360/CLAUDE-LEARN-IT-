## BRIEF STORIES (2-3)

**STORY R8-PS1 — Fifteen Years to Proximity**
Magdalene Wambui left Kenya for Cambodia in 2010. She built a teaching career. She traveled across Asia. After fifteen years, she came back — not because the career ended, but because proximity to her family was the thing the career had been costing her. She now teaches in Tanzania, close enough to home. The covenant with people names this calculation precisely: the cost of keeping people present only via screen across fifteen time zones, and the decision to pay a different cost instead. (Source: Mwakilishi, March 2026)

**STORY R8-PS2 — Trust as Currency in Nairobi**
Laila Macharia, founder of the Aspen Initiative Africa-Nairobi, gave a TEDxNairobi talk in February 2026 on trust as the currency of collective future. She had raised in three countries — Kenya, Namibia, Somalia — and concluded that the healing of the world begins in the trust-building acts of individuals who decide to show up differently. The Nairobi cardiologist who thinks the covenant with people is someone else's assignment is the specific person she was addressing. (Source: TEDxNairobi, February 2026)

**STORY R8-PS3 — The Ashesi Model: We Did It Ourselves**
Patrick Awuah told the Ashesi Class of 2025: he had urged them, when they were admitted, to conduct themselves in such a manner that when it was done, they would say, "We did it ourselves." He had wanted them to be part of the leadership of the institution — not passive recipients of the degree. The covenant with people runs the same instruction: be the kind of presence in your relationships that makes the other person feel, at the end, that they built something together. (Source: Ashesi Commencement, June 2025)
